Research & Evidence

Selected studies, articles, and news coverage on low-dose radiation therapy for osteoarthritis

What the Research Shows

Research on LDRT for osteoarthritis is mixed. Some older sham-controlled trials did not show a clear advantage over sham treatment, while a more recent randomized trial in mild-to-moderate knee osteoarthritis found improved pain and function at four months. Observational studies often report favorable results, but they are more vulnerable to placebo effects and differences in patient selection. Longer-term and joint-specific randomized evidence is still needed.
