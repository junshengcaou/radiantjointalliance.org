How to Ask Your Hospital

1
Call your hospital's main number and ask to be connected to the Radiation Oncology department.
2
Ask: "Do you offer low-dose radiation therapy for osteoarthritis or degenerative joint pain?"
3
Ask your primary care doctor or rheumatologist for a referral to a radiation oncologist to discuss this option.

ℹ

About this directory: We compiled this directory using publicly available hospital information, direct confirmation from providers, and the American Benign Radiotherapy provider directory. Services may change over time, so we recommend contacting the Radiation Oncology department directly before making travel or appointment plans. This directory focuses on low-dose radiation therapy (LDRT) for osteoarthritis and degenerative joint pain, not every type of arthritis.

Please call to confirm service availability.

We build this directory from hospital websites, direct confirmation from radiation oncology programs, physician profiles, news coverage, and social media — but services can change. Confirmed means the program publicly states it offers LDRT for osteoarthritis, or confirmed it directly to us. Reported † means it appears in a directory or was reported by a clinician or patient, but hasn't been independently verified yet. Either way, call the Radiation Oncology department before making travel or referral plans to confirm current availability, physician access, and insurance coverage.

Directory Disclaimer

Hospital services, physician availability, referral requirements, and insurance coverage may change over time. Please call the hospital directly to confirm that LDRT for osteoarthritis is currently offered before making treatment or travel plans.
